Assisted Walk
Assisted Walking and Object Touching Exercise Guide
Purpose:

To improve mobility, balance, and coordination through guided walking and reaching exercises.

Instructions for the Assisting Person:
  1. Provide Support: Stand to the side or slightly behind the patient, offering support under their arm or on their back, depending on their balance needs.
  2. Ensure Safety: Make sure the area is free from obstacles and that there is enough space to walk and maneuver comfortably.
  3. Encourage Proper Posture: Remind the patient to keep their back straight and look forward rather than down to help with balance.
  4. Walking Guidance: Walk slowly alongside the patient, adjusting your speed to match theirs. Provide gentle encouragement to maintain a steady pace.
Instructions for the Patient:
  1. Focus on Each Step: Concentrate on lifting your feet and placing them carefully to maintain balance.
  2. Reaching for the Object: Once in front of the ball or object, extend your arm slowly and steadily to touch it. Engage your core muscles to help with balance.
  3. Return to Neutral Position: After touching the object, carefully bring your arm back and prepare for the next step.
  4. Rest as Needed: Take breaks when necessary, and listen to your body to avoid overexertion.
Important: Always ensure the exercise is done in a safe environment and consult a healthcare professional if there are any concerns or discomfort.
